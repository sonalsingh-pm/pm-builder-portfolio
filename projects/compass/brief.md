# Compass: AI Product Discovery Assistant — Problem Brief

## The Problem

Product discovery done well is a 4 to 8 week process. A PM investigating a new product area must map the user, understand the competitive landscape, size the market opportunity, identify key risks, and synthesize it all into a brief that can survive executive scrutiny. When the stakes are high, thorough discovery is not optional: it is the foundation on which a build decision gets made and funded. The problem is not that PMs cut corners. The problem is that even a diligent, experienced PM working across multiple product areas cannot conduct research at the breadth and currency that good decisions require. Market reports cost thousands of dollars and are often 12 to 18 months out of date. Running parallel discovery on two or three areas simultaneously is nearly impossible for one person to do well. And the volume of available signals, across competitors, customer signals, industry news, and analogous markets, exceeds what any individual can process in a reasonable timeframe.

## Who Has It

Senior and principal PMs at technology companies who own a product area and are regularly asked to evaluate new bets, new markets, or significant feature investments. They have 7 to 15 years of experience and know exactly what rigorous discovery looks like. They are not looking to skip the work. They are looking to do more of it, faster, and at a level of thoroughness their current process cannot sustain. They are the ones preparing the executive brief that determines whether a product gets funded, staffed, and built.

## Current Workaround

- Spend the first week reading broadly: competitor websites, industry analyst blogs, LinkedIn posts from practitioners in the space, and any internal research that exists
- Purchase or request access to market research reports, often finding that the most relevant ones are behind expensive paywalls or 12 to 18 months out of date
- Conduct 5 to 8 customer interviews over 2 to 3 weeks to validate or challenge early hypotheses, scheduling and synthesizing these manually
- Build a competitive landscape tracker in a spreadsheet, manually visiting each competitor's site, pricing page, and App Store reviews
- Attempt to size the market using whatever data is publicly available, often stitching together 3 to 4 sources with different methodologies and time horizons
- Draft the discovery brief across multiple sessions, often rewriting sections as new information surfaces late in the process
- Present to leadership knowing that a more resourced team or a more senior PM might have found signals they missed

## Cost of the Problem

A rigorous discovery process takes 4 to 8 weeks of focused effort. For a PM carrying a full delivery workload in parallel, the calendar time stretches further. The cost is not just time: it is strategic responsiveness. A company that takes 6 weeks to evaluate a market opportunity moves slower than a competitor that can do it in 6 days. The second cost is quality variance: the depth of a discovery brief depends heavily on the individual PM's seniority, their network, and their access to research resources. Two PMs investigating the same opportunity will produce very different outputs. That inconsistency is a risk at the executive level, where decisions are made based on the quality of the brief, not just the quality of the opportunity.

## Early Signal

The market has already validated demand for AI-assisted research. Perplexity reached a reported $9 billion valuation in 2024 on the premise that professionals want synthesized, sourced answers rather than a list of links to investigate manually. PMs specifically have begun using general-purpose AI tools like Claude and ChatGPT to accelerate parts of their discovery work, but in ad hoc ways with no structured workflow and no quality framework to evaluate the output. Specialized tools exist for fragments of the problem: Amplitude for behavioral patterns, Zeda.io for feedback synthesis, Productboard for roadmap integration. None produce a structured, end-to-end discovery brief with explicit confidence scoring and an auditable research process. (Confidence level: market demand validated; PM-specific willingness to pay is assumed and requires validation.)

## Why Now

Multi-agent AI systems can now conduct structured, sequential research workflows that closely mirror what a skilled human researcher does, but in hours rather than weeks. This capability did not exist at production quality before 2024. At the same time, the tools PMs reach for today (Perplexity, ChatGPT) are general-purpose and produce outputs with no quality signal: the PM cannot tell which parts of the output are grounded in strong evidence and which are hallucinated or outdated. An agent-based discovery tool with PM-written evaluation rubrics solves both the speed problem and the reliability problem simultaneously. And for a PM who is building this tool: demonstrating that you can design and ship an agentic AI system is itself the signal that AI-native companies are now screening for when hiring at the principal level.
