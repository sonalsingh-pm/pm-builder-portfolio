# Rules — Confirmed Patterns

These are confirmed patterns learned from real project experience.
They apply across all skills and all projects automatically.
Do not repeat rules that already live in skill files.

---

## Two-Sided Platform Rules
- Always name the buyer and supplier as separate users with separate
  jobs to be done. Never describe them as a single "user."
- Counter metrics are required. Improving one side at the expense
  of the other is not a win.
- Cold start plan is non-negotiable for any marketplace product.
  Supply before demand.

## B2B Enterprise Rules
- Always identify three distinct people: who configures the product,
  who uses it daily, and who benefits from it. In B2B these are
  almost never the same person.
- Name the economic buyer separately from the end user in every PRD.
  The person who signs the contract is not the person who does the work.

## Supplier Onboarding Rules
- Buyer-branded invitations outperform platform-branded invitations.
  Suppliers recognize the buyer, not the network name.
- Compliance requirements must live in a separate layer from
  functional requirements. They change frequently and are
  country-specific.

## Writing Rules
- Specific numbers over adjectives. Always.
- If a claim has no evidence, label it as an assumption explicitly.
